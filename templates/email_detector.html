Phishing Email Identification Guidelines:

  • Exercise heightened scrutiny with messages containing urgent or threatening language designed to elicit immediate action
  • Carefully verify sender email addresses for slight variations or domain inconsistencies
  • Be suspicious of generic greetings such as "Dear Customer" or "Dear User" instead of personalized names
  • Never provide credentials, financial data, or PII in response to unsolicited email requests
  • Scrutinize messages for grammatical errors and unprofessional formatting inconsistent with legitimate correspondence
  • Inspect hyperlink destinations before clicking by hovering to reveal actual URLs
  • Independently verify unexpected requests through official organizational contact channels
