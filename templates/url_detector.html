Phishing URL Identification Guidelines:

  • Verify domain spelling and watch for common typosquatting techniques (e.g., "paypa1.com" vs "paypal.com")
  • Exercise caution with suspicious TLDs such as .tk, .ml, .ga, .cf, .gq commonly used in phishing campaigns
  • Confirm HTTPS protocol and valid SSL certificates for sites requesting sensitive information
  • Avoid clicking shortened URLs (bit.ly, tinyurl) from untrusted sources without preview
  • Inspect for anomalous characters, excessive subdomains, or IP addresses in place of domain names
